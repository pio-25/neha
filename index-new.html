01   About

The combination
is the point.

"The most interesting work lives where creativity and precision refuse to separate. That is the only place worth being."

Neha researches before she sketches. She reads the history of a problem before she proposes a solution. That habit — ground yourself, then act — runs through everything from how she opens a Revit model to how she approaches a new collaboration.

Her mind holds the big idea and the small detail at the same time. She can see the federated model and the single wall junction. The spatial concept and the coordination protocol. The sketch and the specification. This is not a common combination, and she knows it.

Trained as an architect, sharpened as a BIM coordinator. A product of both worlds equally. Neither background cancels the other — together they produce someone who can draw the building and coordinate the data inside it, sketch the concept and write the BEP.

The work right now is about AI visualisation: learning how to direct a generative model with the same care an architect brings to directing a renderer. A creative act with technical precision underneath.

What I believe

Architecture changes how people live and feel in space — not just how buildings perform, but how mornings begin, how conversations happen, how a corridor makes you feel before you know why. That conviction sits underneath all the technical work, even on the days spent resolving clash reports and managing CDE uploads.

The gap between creativity and precision is not a problem to be solved. It is the most interesting place to work.

The role

What the BIM Coordinator actually did

01
MEP model authoring in Revit 2022
Built mechanical and electrical models from scratch. Worksets by system: HVAC ductwork (supply, extract, fresh air), chilled water pipework (4-pipe FCU system), domestic hot and cold water risers, soil and waste drainage at minimum 1:40 falls, LV containment, lighting and small power, DB locations, fire alarm devices.
02
Federated coordination in Navisworks 2022
Weekly federated model from all discipline .nwc cache files. Five clash rule sets per cycle: MECH vs ARCH hard, MECH vs STR hard, MECH vs ELEC hard, 50mm insulation clearance, 300mm maintenance clearance in front of all electrical panels.
03
BCF issue management and resolution tracking
BCF viewpoints for every clash, exported to BIM 360 Issues, assigned to responsible discipline lead with five-day resolution window. Issues closed only after two consecutive clean runs.
04
CDE management in BIM 360 Docs to ISO 19650
Full information workflow: WIP, Shared (S1), Published (A1/A4), Archive. ISO 19650 naming convention on every file: ONG-047-ZZ-00-M3-ME-0001 with revision and status suffix.
05
BEP contribution and TIDP ownership
QA/QC procedures contributed to post-contract BEP. MEP TIDP maintained weekly across 8 months. Weekly QA: open with Revit Audit, clear warnings, purge unused, visual walkthrough, upload with ISO 19650 filename.
The most complex clash

CL-001: basement transfer beam,
Grid C-4

"A 200mm chilled water flow pipe on the correct coordination route was intersecting a 600 by 300mm concrete transfer beam. Hard clash. Zero tolerance. The structural engineer confirmed the beam could not be cored. Two alternative pipe routes were modelled in Revit, checked against the full federated model in Navisworks, and presented at the weekly coordination meeting. The agreed solution dropped the pipe 400mm and routed through a pre-coordinated sleeve in a non-structural wall instead."

Advancing Construction Through
Additive Manufacturing

Systematic literature review (2020-2024) on how BIM and AI can transform additive manufacturing in construction. University of Liverpool, 2024. Distinction awarded.

Institution
University of Liverpool
Grade
Distinction
Method
Systematic Literature Review · Thematic Analysis
Period
2020 - 2024 literature
The question

Why does construction still waste so much?

Construction is one of the world's least efficient industries: high material waste, labour shortages, slow technology adoption. Additive Manufacturing offers a radical alternative. But AM alone is not enough. To scale it, you need BIM to manage the data and AI to optimise the parameters.

Key findings

Five findings that shift the picture

BIM-AM integration is a multiplier
When BIM data pipelines feed directly into AM printing systems, efficiency improves non-linearly.
AI slashes manual modification time
AI-facilitated parameter optimisation reduces time spent adjusting printing paths and material settings.
On-site vs off-site: different strengths
On-site AM suits large infrastructure. Off-site excels in component prefabrication with higher precision.
Printing parameters are everything
Material choice and deposition strategy directly determine structural integrity.
The data exchange gap is the real barrier
The biggest obstacle is not the technology. It is the absence of industry-wide standards for how BIM models communicate with AM systems. This is the finding that earned the Distinction.

"The most powerful insight was not about a technology. It was about a gap. The industry is not missing tools. It is missing the standards that let those tools talk to each other."

Architectural AI Visualisation
Workflow and Practice

An active body of work exploring how AI image and video tools render unbuilt architecture with precision and atmosphere. Prompt patterns, ControlNet workflows, tool comparison across Midjourney, Higgsfield, Stable Diffusion, Runway ML.

Type
Personal portfolio project
Tools
Midjourney · Higgsfield · Stable Diffusion · ControlNet · Runway ML
Focus
Residential · Conceptual · Urban · Material studies
The workflow

Five stages from idea to image

01
Spatial brief — define intent before opening a tool
Typology, atmosphere, materiality, time of day, user experience. This is architectural thinking, not image thinking. The prompt comes last.
02
Structural prompt engineering — layers, not descriptions
Subject → environment → material → lighting → camera → style reference → negative prompts. Each layer adds control. Removing one adds randomness. Both are valid choices.
03
ControlNet — for geometry-critical outputs
A base sketch, floor plan, or 3D model fed into ControlNet (depth map, canny edge, or line-art mode) to constrain the AI's composition to the actual design intent.
04
Iteration — vary, refine, direct
Seeds are fixed, parameters adjusted, inpainting used to correct specific areas without losing what works. Iteration is where craft enters the process.
05
Video extension — Runway ML and Higgsfield
Selected stills extended into short video walkthroughs. Motion, parallax, atmosphere. An image becomes a moment. A moment becomes an experience.
Prompt patterns

The anatomy of a strong architectural prompt

Typology anchor
"Raw board-marked concrete, aged teak cladding" produces far more controlled results than "modern materials". Specificity is control.
Material language
Start with the building type and its key spatial quality. This grounds the AI in architectural convention before adding atmosphere.
Light as a character
"Late afternoon golden hour, long shadows, diffused light through rice paper screens" does more design work than any style reference.
Negative prompts as quality control
Consistently excluding "distorted perspective, floating elements, cartoon, oversaturated" maintains spatial realism.

"The best AI architectural image is not the one the tool chose. It is the one you directed it toward. Prompting is design thinking in a new medium."

Four Studies

VR pavilion in Unity and Gravity Sketch. IoT-BIM digital twin research. MX3D bridge via WAAM. Revit-ArchiCAD interoperability with Solibri and Synchro 4D.

Institution
University of Liverpool
Tools
Unity · Gravity Sketch · 3ds Max · Revit · ArchiCAD · Solibri · Synchro
Study 01

Turkish Pavilion: VR Design in Unity and Gravity Sketch

A 40m² Turkish-themed pavilion connected to a gallery, designed in 3ds Max and Gravity Sketch then brought into Unity as a fully navigable VR environment. Cultural identity expressed through spatial form.

Study 02

IoT and BIM: Buildings that think

Investigation into how IoT-enabled BIM creates digital twins: buildings that monitor themselves in real time, predict maintenance before failures, and automate processes. Case studies: The Edge, Amsterdam; Shanghai Tower.

Study 03

MX3D Bridge: Printing steel in mid-air

The MX3D Bridge: a 30-foot stainless steel pedestrian bridge in Amsterdam printed by a robotic arm using Wire Arc Additive Manufacturing (WAAM). Computational optimisation for printability, material minimisation, structural soundness.

Study 04

Revit vs ArchiCAD: Can they talk?

The same building modelled in Revit and ArchiCAD, IFC transfer fidelity tested, models checked in Solibri, 4D construction sequence run in Synchro. Geometry, editability, and information fidelity compared across platforms.

Fashion Institute
Where fashion finds its form

A 30,000m² campus in Tingre Nagar — circular composition of private, semi-public, and public zones. An iconic, socially engaging landmark for India's growing fashion industry.

Site area
30,000 sqm · FSI 1.1
Tools
AutoCAD · SketchUp · Revit · Lumion
Year
B.Arch 2021
The concept

Three zones, one circular unity

Fashion entails imagination and time, a constantly shifting phenomenon. In architectural terms: a spatial modulation of continuous movement through time and space. The campus divides into three interlocking zones in a circular composition: a private institute core with academic facilities and studios, a semi-public commercial zone with retail and client service areas, and a public plaza with restaurant, kiosks, open discussion areas, and the central unity feature.

The masterplan resolved institutional and commercial parking ratios, recreational space allocations, and all statutory safety codes across a 30,000m² site, 1.5km from the airport and 100m from a bus stop.

"A campus is not a collection of buildings. It is a composition of experiences. The spaces between the buildings matter as much as the buildings themselves."

Neha Nalavde · B.Arch Thesis · 2021
