THE WORKFLOW

Five stages from idea to image

01
Spatial brief — define intent before opening a tool
Typology, atmosphere, materiality, time of day, user experience. This is architectural thinking, not image thinking. The prompt comes last.
02
Structural prompt engineering — layers, not descriptions
Subject → environment → material → lighting → camera → style reference → negative prompts. Each layer adds control. Removing one adds randomness. Both are valid choices.
03
ControlNet — for geometry-critical outputs
A base sketch, floor plan, or 3D model fed into ControlNet (depth map, canny edge, or line-art mode) to constrain the AI's composition to the actual design intent.
04
Iteration — vary, refine, direct
Seeds are fixed, parameters adjusted, inpainting used to correct specific areas without losing what works. Iteration is where craft enters the process.
05
Video extension — Runway ML and Higgsfield
Selected stills extended into short video walkthroughs. Motion, parallax, atmosphere. An image becomes a moment. A moment becomes an experience.

PROMPT PATTERN

The anatomy of a strong architectural prompt

Typology anchor
"Raw board-marked concrete, aged teak cladding" produces far more controlled results than "modern materials". Specificity is control.
Material language
Start with the building type and its key spatial quality. This grounds the AI in architectural convention before adding atmosphere.
Light as a character
"Late afternoon golden hour, long shadows, diffused light through rice paper screens" does more design work than any style reference.
Negative prompts as quality control
Consistently excluding "distorted perspective, floating elements, cartoon, oversaturated" maintains spatial realism.

"The best AI architectural image is not the one the tool chose. It is the one you directed it toward. Prompting is design thinking in a new medium."
