B.Arch Thesis Spatial Design

Fashion Institute
Where fashion finds its form

A 30,000m² campus in Tingre Nagar — circular composition of private, semi-public, and public zones. An iconic, socially engaging landmark for India's growing fashion industry.

SITE AREA
30,000 sqm · FSI 1.1
TOOLS
AutoCAD · SketchUp · Revit · Lumion
YEAR
B.Arch 2021
THE CONCEPT

Three zones, one circular unity

Fashion entails imagination and time, a constantly shifting phenomenon. In architectural terms: a spatial modulation of continuous movement through time and space. The campus divides into three interlocking zones in a circular composition: a private institute core with academic facilities and studios, a semi-public commercial zone with retail and client service areas, and a public plaza with restaurant, kiosks, open discussion areas, and the central unity feature. The masterplan resolved institutional and commercial parking ratios, recreational space allocations, and all statutory safety codes across a 30,000m² site, 1.5km from the airport and 100m from a bus stop.


"A campus is not a collection of buildings. It is a composition of experiences. The spaces between the buildings matter as much as the buildings themselves."

Neha Nalavde · B.Arch Thesis · 2021
